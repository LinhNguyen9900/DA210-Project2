rank_change_by_genre = {}

for genre in df_ranked['genre'].unique():
    genre_df = df_ranked[df_ranked['genre'] == genre].sort_values('startYear')
    ranks = genre_df['rank'].tolist()
    rank_changes = [abs(ranks[i] - ranks[i-1]) for i in range(1, len(ranks))]
    rank_change_by_genre[genre] = sum(rank_changes)

For our project we looked at movie genre popularity from 2015 to 2025. We decided to focus on movies because it allowed for easier analysis as movie seasons and the range in timeline was something we sought to avoid. We also specifically looked at the impact of covid-19 so we decided to look at the span from 2015-2025. We then used inner join to combine data from the ratings, genre, and title_basics tables and selected the information of their titles, start years, genre of movie, and the number of votes they got. We also grouped movies by year to look at analysis of genre popularity over time which we used in our bar chart and line plot data visualizations. The bar chart then built off of this to look at changes each year in popularity of genres allowing us to look what genres did or did not change and the timeline of these changes. And lastly our pie chart allowed us to look at overall trends within our data in regards to movie genre popularity.

Analysis of movie genre preferences from 2015 to 2025 reveals several notable trends, particularly around the COVID-19 pandemic period. As shown in the pie chart, drama, documentary, and comedy dominate the overall genre distribution, collectively accounting for more than half of all movies in terms of popularity. The line and bar graphs further illustrate how certain genres experienced significant shifts in popularity over the decade. For example, documentary and family genres saw marked improvements in their rankings, especially in the years following the pandemic, while traditional blockbuster genres like action and adventure declined in relative popularity. These findings align with the results of Kubrak (2023), who found that the pandemic led to increased movie-watching frequency, with viewers often gravitating toward genres that provided emotional comfort or escapism, such as documentaries and family films. The data also support Kubraks observation that psychological factors, such as anxiety and avoidance coping strategies, played a key role in shaping genre preferences during the pandemic, as audiences sought content that reflected their experiences or offered relief from real-world stressors.
